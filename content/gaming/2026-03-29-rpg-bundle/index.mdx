The Humble Bundle features games developed by Owlcat Studios and Beamdog, including heavy-hitters like Planescape: Torment and Baldur’s Gate II: Enhanced Edition. This collection packs 12 PC games, with the entry-level tier starting at $5 for four games. According to [GameSpot](https://www.gamespot.com/), the full bundle containing all 12 items is available for $23. The bundle supports the charity DonorsChoose, which provides materials and facilities for classrooms across America.


The pricing model allows customers to choose how much to pay, with higher payments unlocking more games and increasing charity contributions. As [Humble Bundle](https://www.humblebundle.com/) notes, the more you pay, the more games you get and the more money goes to charity. This approach has been well-received by the community, with many players appreciating the flexibility in pricing.


The community is responding positively to the bundle, with many players noting the value for money and the quality of the games included. As [Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/) users discuss, the bundle is a great opportunity for new players to try out classic CRPGs and for experienced players to complete their collections. With the bundle including [Rogue Trader](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1066180/Rogue_Trader/) and other notable titles, it's clear that the community is excited about the offering.

<ArticleBreak />

For all the noise, the actual story is about the games and the community. The bundle is advertised as containing $296 worth of games, which is a significant value for players. As [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/) reviews show, the games included in the bundle have received high praise from players, with many considering them to be classics in the CRPG genre.


The real signal here is that the community is looking for value and quality in their gaming experiences. With the rise of game bundles and subscription services, players are increasingly expecting more for their money. As [PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/) notes, the RPG genre is particularly well-suited to bundles, with many classic games still holding up today.


The bundle's support for DonorsChoose is also a significant factor, with many players appreciating the opportunity to give back to the community. As [DonorsChoose](https://www.donorschoose.org/) themselves note, the charity provides essential materials and facilities for classrooms across America, making a real difference in the lives of students and teachers.
