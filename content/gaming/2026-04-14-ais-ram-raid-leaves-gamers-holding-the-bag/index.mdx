AYANEO just pulled the plug on its NEXT 2 Windows gaming handheld, and the reason isn’t some niche manufacturing hiccup—it’s the AI boom. The company confirmed sales are suspended because RAM and SSD shortages, fueled by insatiable demand from AI data centers, have made production "no longer sustainable." That’s not just bad news for AYANEO; it’s a warning shot for anyone eyeing high-end handheld PCs like the [Steam Deck OLED](https://www.steamdeck.com/en/oled) or [ROG Ally](https://rog.asus.com/gaming-handhelds/rog-ally/).


The NEXT 2 wasn’t some budget device—it was an enthusiast-class machine with specs meant to rival the best in the space. But when AI workloads start hoarding hardware, even premium gaming gear gets caught in the collateral damage. According to [Windows Central](https://www.windowscentral.com), the shortages aren’t just temporary; they’re a structural shift in the supply chain. If you were waiting for a price drop or a revised model, don’t hold your breath. The community is already bracing for delays, with some Reddit threads speculating that other manufacturers could face similar issues.


This isn’t just about one handheld. It’s about a market where AI’s hunger for hardware is reshaping priorities. Gamers, who’ve spent years dealing with GPU shortages and scalper prices, are now watching as RAM and SSDs—once plentiful—become the new battleground. The question isn’t whether this will affect other devices; it’s how long until it does.

<ArticleBreak />

The real kicker? This shortage isn’t just a blip. Early signals suggest the AI industry’s demand for high-capacity RAM and SSDs will only grow, and gaming hardware is now competing with data centers for the same components. Players are already feeling the pinch, with some Steam forums noting that even mid-range handhelds could see price hikes or limited stock. The community’s reaction has been a mix of frustration and dark humor—memes about AI "stealing" their gaming rigs are circulating, but the underlying anxiety is real.


AYANEO hasn’t given a timeline for resuming sales, and that silence speaks volumes. If the shortages persist, we could see a ripple effect: fewer high-end handhelds, longer wait times, and potentially higher prices. For now, gamers are left with a stark choice: pay a premium for existing stock or wait—and hope the AI industry doesn’t swallow the entire supply chain whole.


The irony? While gamers debate frame rates and ray tracing, the biggest threat to their hobby might not be a bad patch or a delayed game—it’s the hardware they can’t even buy. The NEXT 2’s suspension is a canary in the coal mine, and the warning is clear: the AI boom isn’t just changing tech; it’s changing what’s available to play it on.
