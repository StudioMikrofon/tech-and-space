Valve is releasing a native Steam Link app for Apple's Vision Pro, enabling wireless streaming of Steam library games from a nearby Mac or PC over a local network. This move could pave the way for future VR game support on Vision Pro, though Valve has not confirmed this yet. According to [Steam News](https://store.steampowered.com/news), the app only supports traditional 2D games, not VR games.


The community is responding positively to the news, with some users noting that this could be a convenient feature for Vision Pro users to access their Steam libraries wirelessly. As [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/) reports, the update is positioned as a convenience feature for Vision Pro users, akin to existing Steam Link platforms.

The real signal here is that Valve is committed to expanding its gaming ecosystem to new platforms. As [Wired](https://www.wired.com/) notes, the company has previously released Steam Link apps for Mac, iPhone, iPad, and Apple TV, with local network streaming and controller sync capabilities. It's possible that the Vision Pro app may inherit controller sync functionality from prior Steam Link versions, though this is not explicitly stated.
