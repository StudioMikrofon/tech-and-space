Capcom has announced it will not use AI-generated assets in its games. This decision comes after investor communications, where the company stated its commitment to human-created content. According to [PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/capcom-says-it-will-not-implement-assets-generated-by-ai-into-our-game-content/), Capcom plans to use AI to enhance efficiency and boost productivity in game development. 


The community is responding to this news, with some players noting the potential benefits of AI in game development, such as improved graphics and faster development times. However, others are concerned about the potential risks of relying on AI, including the loss of jobs and the homogenization of game content. [Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/gaming/) users are discussing the implications of this decision, with some speculating about the potential impact on the gaming industry as a whole.

The distinction between 'assets' and 'development tools' is implied but not explicitly defined by Capcom. This lack of clarity has led to speculation about what exactly constitutes an 'AI-generated asset'. Some players are wondering if this means that AI will not be used in any aspect of game development, or if there are certain exceptions. The use of AI in game development is a complex issue, with both potential benefits and risks. As the gaming industry continues to evolve, it will be important to monitor how companies like Capcom balance the use of AI with the need for human-created content.
