For a game built by ex-*StarCraft* and *Warcraft* veterans, losing online play isn’t just a technical hiccup—it’s a gut punch to the genre’s core. Frost Giant’s [unannounced RTS](https://frostgiant.com/) (still in closed testing) relied on Improbable’s SpatialOS for its netcode, but the latter’s [acquisition by AI firm Inworld](https://techcrunch.com/2024/05/15/inworld-acquires-improbable/) left the studio scrambling. ‘We hope to restore online play in a future patch,’ the team [told *GamesRadar+*](https://www.gamesradar.com/frost-giant-rts-online-features-lost/), which translates to: *‘We’re figuring this out as we go.’*


The timing stings extra hard because this isn’t just any RTS—it’s from the team behind *Warcraft III*’s golden era, a game whose [disastrous modern relaunch](https://www.pcgamer.com/warcraft-3-reforged-is-getting-a-major-overhaul/) still haunts Blizzard. Players were already nervous about Frost Giant’s ability to deliver a true successor to *StarCraft II*’s competitive scene. Now, the [Steam forums](https://steamcommunity.com/app/XXXXX/discussions/) are split between ‘wait and see’ optimism and ‘here we go again’ cynicism, with one top comment summing it up: *‘So it’s Warcraft III PT 2: Electric Boogaloo?’*


**PATCH TRANSLATOR**: Right now, this means no ranked ladders, no co-op vs. AI, and no custom games—the lifeblood of RTS longevity. Single-player and LAN might still work, but for a genre that thrives on [1v1 tension and esports dreams](https://liquipedia.net/starcraft2/Main_Page), that’s like selling a racecar without wheels.

<ArticleBreak />

**COMMUNITY PULSE**: The loudest voices aren’t the angriest—they’re the ones laughing through the pain. Reddit’s [r/Starcraft](https://www.reddit.com/r/starcraft/) is flooding with memes comparing this to *Diablo Immortal*’s ‘Don’t you guys have phones?’ moment, but the deeper concern is whether Frost Giant can avoid Blizzard’s [infamous ‘we’ll fix it later’](https://kotaku.com/blizzard-warcraft-3-reforged-disaster-1842627705) cycle. Some testers report the single-player campaign feels polished, but without multiplayer, that’s like praising a MMORPG for its character creator.


The real friction point isn’t the delay—it’s the radio silence on *how*. Frost Giant’s [latest update](https://frostgiant.com/news) avoids specifics: no backup server plans, no ETA, just ‘hope.’ That’s a risky play when your audience includes *StarCraft* pros who’ve spent decades dissecting patch notes for hidden meanings. If this drags on, the game risks launching as a *‘play it later’* promise, and RTS fans know how that story ends: [see: *Command & Conquer: Rivals*](https://www.ea.com/games/command-and-conquer/command-and-conquer-rivals).


**BACKLASH RADAR**: The danger isn’t backlash—it’s apathy. RTS players are used to [abandoned projects](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/articles/2023-01-10-where-did-all-the-rts-games-go) and half-baked revivals. If Frost Giant can’t restore online play *before* open beta, the community’s patience will evaporate faster than a Zerg rush.
