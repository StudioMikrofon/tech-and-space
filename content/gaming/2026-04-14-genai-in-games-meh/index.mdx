Circana's PlayerPulse survey found that just over 25% of US video game players said a game using generative AI would make them less likely to buy it. The majority were neutral or unsure, according to Circana's Mat Piscatella. This indifference is interesting, given the [vocal opposition](https://www.gamespot.com/articles/genai-in-games-most-players-just-dont-care-study/1100-6512936/) from some gamers.


A [growing share of players](https://www.circana.com/) say they are more likely than before to refuse to buy a game if it uses generative AI. However, the study highlights that most gamers are indifferent to genAI in games, with no strong preference either for or against it. The [community reaction](https://www.reddit.com/r/gaming/) is mixed, with some players expressing concerns about the impact of genAI on gameplay and others seeing it as a non-issue.


The [use of genAI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_ai) in games is still a relatively new phenomenon, and it's unclear how it will affect the gaming industry in the long run. However, one thing is clear: the vocal minority opposed to genAI is making a lot of noise, while the majority of players remain undecided. As the [industry continues to evolve](https://www.theverge.com/), it will be interesting to see how genAI is integrated into games and how players respond to it.

The [community pulse](https://www.steam.com/) on genAI in games is complex, with some players expressing enthusiasm for the technology and others voicing concerns. However, the majority of players seem to be taking a wait-and-see approach, neither strongly supporting nor opposing the use of genAI. As [GameSpot](https://www.gamespot.com/) notes, the vocal minority opposed to genAI is making a lot of noise, but it's unclear whether this opposition will have a significant impact on the gaming industry.


The [backlash radar](https://www.pcgamer.com/) is on high alert, with some players worried that genAI could lead to a homogenization of gameplay or a loss of creativity in game development. However, others see genAI as a tool that can enhance gameplay and provide new experiences for players. As the [industry continues to explore](https://www.ign.com/) the potential of genAI, it's likely that we'll see a mix of both positive and negative outcomes.


The [player expectation](https://www.eurogamer.net/) is that genAI will be used in a way that enhances gameplay and provides new experiences, rather than simply replacing human developers. However, it's unclear whether this expectation will be met, and the [leak credibility](https://www.kotaku.com/) of rumors about genAI in games is always a concern. As the [gaming community](https://www.gamasutra.com/) continues to discuss and debate the use of genAI, it's likely that we'll see a range of different opinions and perspectives on the issue.
