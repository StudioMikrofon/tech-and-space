The world of Pokémon is vast and wondrous, with a plethora of monsters to catch and train. However, despite the many games and generations, Game Freak has never used 9 different type combinations in a Pokémon game. This raises an interesting question: what are the odds of a Fire/Fairy Pombon versus a Fire/Fighting Pombon? According to [Polygon](https://www.polygon.com/), these unseen combinations could potentially shake up the meta and bring new strategies to the table. The community is responding with a mix of excitement and skepticism, with some players noting that these new combinations could be a game-changer, while others are more cautious in their expectations. As [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/) notes, the introduction of new types and combinations can have a significant impact on the gameplay and balance of the game.

So, what does this mean for actual players in actual gameplay? For one, it could mean a whole new level of strategy and depth to the game. Players would need to adapt to new strengths and weaknesses, and potentially rethink their entire team composition. As [Wired](https://www.wired.com/) suggests, this could lead to a more dynamic and engaging gameplay experience. On the other hand, there's also the risk of imbalance and overpowered combinations, which could lead to a negative player experience. The community is already discussing the potential implications, with some players expressing concern about the potential backlash. As [Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/) users note, the key to success will be in the execution and balance of these new combinations.
