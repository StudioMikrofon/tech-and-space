Google and Amazon have come under fire for their involvement in Project Nimbus, a cloud computing contract with Israel's Ministry of Defense and the Israeli Security Agency. The Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) has urged both companies to uphold their human rights commitments and increase transparency. Despite [CONFIRMED] evidence of serious risk, both companies have refused to take action. Amazon has completely ignored the EFF's original and follow-up letters, while Google has repeatedly promised to respond but has not taken meaningful action over more than a year and a half.


The [LIKELY] involvement of Google Cloud and AWS in providing cloud computing services to Israel's Ministry of Defense and Shin Bet raises human rights concerns. The EFF's concerns have been reinforced by additional reporting since late 2024, highlighting the need for greater transparency and accountability. As noted by the [EFF](https://www.eff.org/), the lack of action by Google and Amazon suggests potential complicity in human rights risks tied to their cloud contracts with Israeli government agencies.

<ArticleBreak />

The everyday impact of this issue is significant, as it affects not only the companies involved but also the individuals and communities who may be harmed by their actions. The [COMMUNITY] is responding with concern, noting that the companies' public human rights commitments are not being upheld. As [reported](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2022/sep/14/google-amazon-project-nimbus-israel) by The Guardian, the companies' responses (or lack thereof) are inadequate given their stated ethical and transparency policies.


The real signal here is that the companies are prioritizing their business interests over their human rights commitments. This raises questions about the direction of change and which values will win out in the end. As [noted](https://www.wired.com/story/google-amazon-project-nimbus/) by Wired, the issue is not just about Google and Amazon, but about the broader implications for human rights and accountability in the tech industry.
