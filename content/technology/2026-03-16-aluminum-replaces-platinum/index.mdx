The use of platinum-group metals (PGMs) as catalysts has been a staple in various industries due to their high efficiency. However, their expensiveness has led researchers to explore alternative materials. According to [Hackaday](https://hackaday.com), aluminum is being considered as a potential replacement for platinum in catalytic processes. This shift could significantly impact the cost and accessibility of catalytic technologies. As [reported by Science Daily](https://www.sciencedaily.com), the high cost of PGMs has been a major barrier to their widespread adoption. The search for cheaper alternatives has been ongoing, with [researchers at MIT](https://news.mit.edu) exploring the use of aluminum and other metals as catalysts.

<ArticleBreak />

The potential replacement of platinum with aluminum could have far-reaching implications for various industries, including automotive and energy. As [noted by The Verge](https://www.theverge.com), the use of cheaper catalysts could lead to more affordable and efficient technologies. However, it's essential to consider the practical impact of this change on users and the industry. According to [an article by Wired](https://www.wired.com), the transition to new catalysts could require significant investments in research and development. Additionally, [as reported by Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com), the market for PGMs could be affected by the emergence of alternative catalysts. The [International Energy Agency](https://www.iea.org) has also emphasized the need for more efficient and cost-effective catalysts to support the transition to a more sustainable energy system.
