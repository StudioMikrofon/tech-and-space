Apple's online store in the U.S. is currently showing delivery estimates of up to 4-5 months for many Mac mini and Mac Studio configurations with upgraded amounts of RAM. The delays are occurring amid a severe global memory chip shortage driven by surging demand from companies building AI servers that require large amounts of RAM. For example, a Mac mini with an M4 Pro chip and 64GB of RAM ordered from Apple's online store in the U.S. today is estimated to ship in 16-18 weeks. 


Even the $599 base model with an M4 chip and 16GB of RAM has a one-month delay. This suggests that the shortage is not just limited to high-end configurations, but is affecting the entire product line. According to [MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/), the shortage is severe and industry-wide, affecting Apple's supply chain.

<ArticleBreak />

The impact of these delays on users and the industry cannot be overstated. Many businesses and individuals rely on Mac minis and Mac Studios for their work, and the inability to get these machines in a timely manner can cause significant disruptions. As noted by [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/), the shortage is also affecting other companies in the tech industry, highlighting the need for a more diversified and resilient supply chain. 


In terms of what works and what doesn't, it's clear that the current supply chain is not equipped to handle the demand for these machines. However, Apple's efforts to prioritize orders and provide timely updates on availability are a step in the right direction. As reported by [Wired](https://www.wired.com/), the company is also exploring alternative sources for its components, which could help alleviate the shortage in the long term.
