Google is announcing a new version of Android Automotive that goes beyond infotainment use cases to control more of the car. This change represents a significant expansion of the operating system's capabilities. According to [9to5Google](https://9to5google.com), previous versions were primarily limited to infotainment functions. 


The new version is designed to control more of the car, which could have a significant impact on the user experience. For example, [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com) notes that this could enable more integrated and seamless interactions between the driver and the vehicle. 


This expansion of Android Automotive's capabilities is likely to have a significant impact on the industry, with [Wired](https://www.wired.com) suggesting that it could lead to more competition in the market for in-car operating systems.

<ArticleBreak />

The practical impact of this change will be significant, as it will enable drivers to control more of their car's functions from a single interface. This could lead to increased efficiency and safety, as drivers will not have to switch between multiple systems to control different functions. 


However, there are also potential drawbacks to this expansion, such as the potential for increased complexity and distractions while driving. As [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com) notes, the key to success will be to design an interface that is intuitive and easy to use, while also minimizing distractions. 


The ecosystem effects of this change will also be significant, as it will enable more integration with other Android devices and services. For example, [Android Authority](https://www.androidauthority.com) suggests that this could enable more seamless interactions between the car and other Android devices, such as smartphones and tablets.
