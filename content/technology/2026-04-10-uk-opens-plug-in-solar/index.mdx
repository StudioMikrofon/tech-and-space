Experts from UK-based consultancy Ricardo, part of the WSP Group, authored a column for PV magazine regarding plug-in solar panels. According to available information, the UK will soon allow the installation of plug-in solar panels under 800 W without the need for a qualified electrician. This change is intended to make home solar more affordable and accessible, as [explained by PV Magazine](https://www.pvmagazine.com/). 


 The systems are characterized as low-cost and portable, which could significantly boost household solar adoption. As [noted by the UK government](https://www.gov.uk/), the country aims to increase its renewable energy growth, and these new systems will complement this effort. Portable plug-in solar systems are expected to be available for sale by summer 2026, [according to reports](https://www.theguardian.com/).

<ArticleBreak />

The practical impact of this change is substantial, as it will allow more households to install solar panels without the need for a qualified electrician. This could lead to a significant increase in the adoption of home solar systems, as the cost and complexity of installation are reduced. As [discussed by industry experts](https://www.renewableenergyworld.com/), the market context for solar energy is becoming increasingly competitive, with key players such as [Tesla](https://www.tesla.com/) and [Vivint Solar](https://www.vivintsolar.com/) offering innovative solutions. 


 The ecosystem effects of this change will be far-reaching, as it will affect not only households but also the wider renewable energy industry. The second-order impact of this change could be a behavior change, as more people become aware of the benefits of solar energy and begin to adopt it. As [reported by Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/), the demand for renewable energy is increasing globally, and this change could contribute to this trend.
