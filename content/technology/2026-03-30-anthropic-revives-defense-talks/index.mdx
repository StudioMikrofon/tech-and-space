Anthropic is reportedly trying to reach a new deal with the US Defense Department, which could prevent the government from labeling it a supply chain risk. According to [Financial Times](https://www.ft.com/) and [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/), Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei has resumed talks with the agency over the use of its AI models. In particular, the publications say that Amodei is having discussions with Emil Michael, the Under Secretary of Defense for Research and Engineering.


The two of them were trying to work out the contract over the use of Anthropic’s models before negotiations broke down and the government soured on the company. This development has significant implications for the tech industry, as it may set a precedent for how AI companies interact with government agencies. As [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/) notes, the US government has been increasingly cautious about partnering with AI companies due to concerns over data security and surveillance.

<ArticleBreak />

The renewed discussions between Anthropic and the Defense Department may be a sign that the company is willing to address the government's concerns and find a mutually beneficial agreement. However, as [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/) points out, the deal could also have significant ecosystem effects, potentially impacting other AI companies and their relationships with government agencies. The [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/) community is watching the developments closely, as they may have far-reaching consequences for the industry.


The practical impact of this change is that it could lead to increased collaboration between AI companies and government agencies, potentially driving innovation and progress in the field. However, it also raises important questions about data security, surveillance, and the responsible use of AI technology. As the industry continues to evolve, it will be essential to balance the benefits of AI with the need for transparency, accountability, and ethical considerations.
