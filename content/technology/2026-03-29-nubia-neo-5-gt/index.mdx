Nubia unveiled the nubia Neo 5 GT at MWC, a gaming phone with a built-in cooling fan and RGB lighting. The phone will be available globally at a price of €400, with Southeast Asia being the first region to receive it. According to [GSMArena](https://www.gsmarena.com/), the nubia Neo 5 GT is the first in its class with a built-in cooling fan. This feature is typically found in more expensive gaming phones, such as the RedMagic 11 Air, which launched earlier this year with a Snapdragon 8 Elite chipset and a 6.85” 144Hz display priced at €500.


The nubia Neo 5 GT's cooling fan is a significant feature, as it allows for more intense gaming sessions without overheating. However, the specific chipset used in the nubia Neo 5 GT is not specified, which could be a concern for some users. As noted by [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/), the choice of chipset can greatly impact a phone's performance and battery life.

The launch of the nubia Neo 5 GT is significant, as it brings a high-end gaming feature to a more affordable price point. As [Wired](https://www.wired.com/) notes, gaming phones with built-in cooling fans are becoming more and more affordable, which could change the way users approach mobile gaming. The nubia Neo 5 GT's €400 price point makes it an attractive option for users who want a high-performance gaming phone without breaking the bank.


The nubia Neo 5 GT's impact on the industry will depend on its performance and how it compares to other gaming phones on the market. As [TechRadar](https://www.techradar.com/) points out, the gaming phone market is highly competitive, and manufacturers are constantly looking for ways to differentiate their products. The nubia Neo 5 GT's built-in cooling fan and RGB lighting may be enough to set it apart from other phones in its class.
