SpaceX is set to launch 29 Starlink satellites on a Falcon 9 rocket from Cape Canaveral Space Force Station. The launch, scheduled for 10:47 a.m. EDT, will take place from pad 40. According to [Spaceflight Now](https://spaceflightnow.com/), the mission is intended to expand the Starlink satellite constellation. The Starlink program aims to provide global internet connectivity through a network of low-Earth orbit satellites.


The Falcon 9 rocket will fly on a northeasterly trajectory, carrying the 29 Starlink satellites into orbit. This launch is part of a larger effort by SpaceX to establish a constellation of thousands of satellites. As noted by [NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/), the Starlink program has the potential to revolutionize global communication.

<ArticleBreak />

The launch of the Starlink 10-62 mission marks an important milestone in the development of the Starlink constellation. With this launch, SpaceX will bring the total number of Starlink satellites in orbit to over 3,000. As reported by [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/), the Starlink program has faced challenges and controversies, including concerns over the impact of the satellites on astronomical research. However, the program also has the potential to provide internet access to underserved communities around the world.


The success of the Starlink program will depend on the ability of SpaceX to launch and operate a large number of satellites. As noted by [Space News](https://spacenews.com/), the company has made significant investments in the development of the Starlink program. The launch of the Starlink 10-62 mission is an important step towards achieving the goals of the program.
