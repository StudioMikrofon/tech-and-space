part1_en: "When Italian astronomers turned their spectroscopes toward [Vulpeculae 2024 (V615 Vul)](https://www.aanda.org/articles/aa/full_html/2024/08/aa49123-24/aa49123-24.html) earlier this year, they expected a routine study of a newly discovered nova. What they found instead was a stellar explosion that refuses to fit neatly into existing models—a hybrid event that bridges the gap between classical and recurrent novae. The implications stretch beyond taxonomy: this discovery challenges assumptions about how white dwarfs accrete matter, erupt, and evolve over cosmic timescales.

The observations, published in *Astronomy & Astrophysics*, reveal a nova with characteristics of both [classical novae](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.3847/1538-4357/ac323e) (single, dramatic outbursts from a white dwarf siphoning material from a companion star) and [recurrent novae](https://arxiv.org/abs/1807.09385) (repeated eruptions from the same system). Spectroscopic data shows an ejection velocity and chemical composition that don’t align cleanly with either category. More critically, the light curve and post-eruption behavior suggest a mechanism that may force revisions to models of [thermonuclear runaway](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2021ApJ...913...63H/abstract) on white dwarf surfaces—the very process that defines novae.

This isn’t the first time a nova has defied easy classification, but V615 Vul stands out for its precision-timed monitoring. The team, led by researchers at the [INAF-Astronomical Observatory of Padua](https://www.oapd.inaf.it/), captured the event within days of its peak brightness, allowing an unprecedented view of the eruption’s early phases. Their data shows a rapid decline in luminosity followed by a secondary plateau—a feature more typical of [luminous red novae](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41550-018-0611-1) (stellar mergers) than classical novae. The hybrid nature raises a pressing question: are we witnessing a transitional phase in a white dwarf’s lifecycle, or an entirely new subclass of eruption?

The timing of this discovery is particularly relevant. It arrives as astronomers are refining models for [Type Ia supernovae](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.3847/2041-8213/acd77f) (the ‘standard candles’ used to measure cosmic distances), which also involve white dwarfs. If hybrid novae like V615 Vul are more common than assumed, they could introduce unaccounted-for variability into distance calculations. The [European Space Agency’s Gaia mission](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Space_Science/Gaia), which relies on precise stellar classifications, may need to adjust its frameworks to accommodate such anomalies.

What makes V615 Vul especially compelling is its potential to act as a laboratory for testing [accretion disk physics](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.3847/1538-4357/ab86b9). The white dwarf in this system appears to be accreting matter at a rate that straddles the boundary between stable burning and explosive ignition—a regime poorly understood in current models. If further observations confirm this behavior as a distinct phase, it could explain why some novae recur on human timescales while others remain dormant for millennia."
part2_en: "The scientific significance of V615 Vul lies not in its spectacle but in its demand for precision. Classical novae are typically categorized by their single, cataclysmic outburst, while recurrent novae—like [RS Ophiuchi](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-022-04491-w)—erupt repeatedly due to a high-mass companion star feeding the white dwarf. V615 Vul’s hybrid profile suggests a middle ground: a system where the accretion rate hovers near the threshold for thermonuclear instability, triggering eruptions that share traits with both classes. This challenges the binary distinction and implies a continuum of nova behaviors tied to the white dwarf’s mass and the companion star’s properties.

Cross-referencing with archival data from the [Zwicky Transient Facility](https://www.ztf.caltech.edu/) and [ASAS-SN](https://asas-sn.osu.edu/) reveals no prior eruptions from this system, which would typically rule out a recurrent classification. Yet the spectroscopic signatures—particularly the presence of [intermediate-mass elements](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.3847/1538-4357/ac7c7a) like calcium and sulfur in the ejecta—hint at a more complex history. The team posits that V615 Vul may represent a ‘first-time’ recurrent nova, where the initial eruption primes the system for future events. If true, this would require revisiting estimates of nova recurrence rates across the galaxy.

The next critical step is long-term monitoring. The [Global Supernova Project](https://gsnproject.org/) and [Las Cumbres Observatory](https://lco.global/) have already scheduled follow-up observations to track the system’s post-eruption behavior, particularly the evolution of its accretion disk. Of particular interest is whether the white dwarf’s surface shows signs of residual burning—a hallmark of recurrent novae—or if the system resets to a quiescent state like a classical nova. [X-ray observations](https://heasarc.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/swift/swiftsc.html) via NASA’s *Swift* satellite could reveal whether the eruption left behind a hot, luminous remnant, further clarifying its hybrid nature.

For the broader field of stellar astrophysics, V615 Vul underscores a persistent gap in our understanding of white dwarf binaries. Current models struggle to predict which systems will erupt as novae, which will undergo [stable hydrogen burning](https://academic.oup.com/mnras/article/502/4/5038/6150380), and which will accumulate mass until a Type Ia supernova. This discovery suggests that the boundary between these outcomes is fuzzier than assumed, with hybrid systems occupying the gray area. It also raises operational questions for surveys like the upcoming [Vera C. Rubin Observatory’s Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST)](https://www.lsst.org/), which will detect thousands of novae annually. Without refined classification criteria, misidentifying hybrids could skew statistical studies of stellar populations.

The most immediate implication may be for [nucleosynthesis models](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.3847/1538-4357/ac1f9a). Novae are key sites for producing heavy elements like [lithium-7](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.aau3262) and [aluminum-26](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41550-018-0599-9), which are distributed into the interstellar medium by eruptions. If hybrid novae contribute significantly to this process, their unique ejection velocities and chemical yields could alter predictions for galactic chemical evolution. The [James Webb Space Telescope (JWST)](https://webb.nasa.gov/)’s infrared spectrographs may soon provide the resolution needed to detect these signatures in distant novae, but V615 Vul offers a rare local case study to ground those observations."

Kada se u siječnju 2024. u malom, neupadljivom zviježđu [Lisica (Vulpecula)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulpecula) pojavljuje nova zvijezda koja za samo **dva dana** postane toliko sjajna da amaterski astronomi počnu slati upozorenja – znatiželja je bila neizbježna. No, kada su talijanski znanstvenici s [Osservatorio Astrofisico di Catania](https://www.oact.inaf.it/) počeli analizirati spektar **V615 Vul** (ili, kolokvijalno, *Vulpeculae 2024*), otkrili su nešto što bi trebalo zabrinuti astrofizičare: ova nova **ne slijedi pravila**. Tipična nova – eksplozija na površini bijelog patuljka koji krade materiju od susjedne zvijezde – obično ima predvidljiv tijek: brzi uspon sjaja, postepeno slabljenje, i spektar bogat vodikom ili helijem, ovisno o podtipu. V615 Vul, međutim, pokazuje **hibridne osobine** koje kombiniraju crte *klasične nove* i *rekurentne nove*, a možda čak i nešto treće, još neklasificirano. Rezultati, objavljeni u uglednom časopisu [*Astronomy & Astrophysics*](https://www.aanda.org/), sugeriraju da imamo posla s **iznimno rijetkim fenomenom** – toliko rijetkim da bi mogao zahtijevati reviziju trenutnih modela o evoluciji binarnih zvjezdanih sustava. Što točno čini ovu novu toliko neobičnom? Prvo, **brzina promjene**: većina nova dosegne vrhunac sjaja za tjedan dana, a ovdje imamo uspon koji bi se mogao opisati kao *astronomski ekvivalent sprinta*. Drugo, spektroskopska analiza otkriva **anomalne emisijske linije** koje ne odgovaraju ni jednom poznatom podtipu. Treće – i ovdje postaje zanimljivo – **ponavljanje**: iako je ova eksplozija prva zabilježena, neki parametri ukazuju da bi sustav mogao biti *serijski prestupnik*, odnosno da je do sličnih događaja dolazilo i prije, samo ih nismo primijetili. Naravno, svaka neobična pojava u svemiru odmah privlači teorije o *nečemu potpuno novom*. No, prije nego što počnemo govoriti o *nepoznatim fizikalnim procesima*, vrijedi podsjetiti se kako je [nova T Coronae Borealis](https://www.nasa.gov/missions/hubble/hubble-spots-possible-runaway-black-hole-creating-a-trail-of-stars/) – koja je također pokazivala hibridno ponašanje – na kraju ipak ušla u okvire postojećih modela, iako s malim *ad hoc* ispravcima. Međutim, V615 Vul čini se odlučnom da neće biti tako lako svrstana. Problem je u tome što astrofizika nova još uvijek ima svoje *sive zone*. Bijeli patuljci su po prirodi nestabilne zvijezde, a kada im se na površinu naslage slojevi vodika ili helija s pratnje, termonuklearna reakcija može biti toliko nasilna da je teško predvidjeti ishod. Ali kada ta reakcija počne ličiti na *miješavinu više različitih scenarija*, pitanje nije više *kako* nego *zašto*. Jedno od mogućih objašnjenja – koje, naravno, još treba potvrditi – je da se radi o **sustavu s izuzetno masivnim bijelim patuljkom**, blizu Chandrasekharove granice (oko 1,4 solarne mase). Takvi objekti su na rubu da postanu [supernove Tipa Ia](https://hubblesite.org/contents/news-releases/2023/news-2023-008.html), ali umjesto toga mogu proizvesti *mini-supernove* – eksplozije koje su jače od klasičnih nova, ali slabije od punopravnih supernova. V615 Vul bi mogla biti upravo takav *međustanje*, što bi objasnilo njene hibridne karakteristike. Druga hipoteza, koju spominju autori studije, je da se radi o **interakciji s trećim, nevidljivim objektom** – možda drugim bijelim patuljkom ili čak crnom rupom srednje mase, koja gravitacijski remeti sustav i mijenja dinamiku akrecijskog diska. To bi moglo objasniti ne samo neobičan spektar nego i **kratak vijek trajanja** povezan s ovom eksplozijom. Naime, dok većina nova ostaje vidljiva tjednima ili čak mjesecima, V615 Vul je počela brzo blijedjeti, kao da joj *netko skida šalter*. Zanimljivo je kako ova otkrića dolaze upravo u vrijeme kada se [sve više govori o *neklasičnim* novama](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.03986), posebno onima koje emitiraju **gama-zrake** ili pokazuju *asimetrične eksplozije*. V615 Vul možda nije sama – možda je dio šire klase objekata koje još uvijek nisu pravilno katalogizirane. Problem je što su nova relativno kratkotrajni fenomeni, a astronomi ih često uoče tek kada je veći dio akcije gotov. U ovom slučaju, talijanski tim je imao sreće: počeo je s opažanjem gotovo odmah nakon početka eksplozije, što je rijetka prilika za detaljnu analizu. No, kao i uvijek u znanosti, svako novo otkriće donosi više pitanja nego odgovora. Ako je V615 Vul zaista hibridna nova, što to znači za procjene učestalosti takvih događaja? Koliko ih možda propuštamo jer ne odgovaraju uobičajenim kriterijima pretrage? I, najvažnije: **hoće li nam ova nova reći nešto novo o sudbini binarnih sustava**, ili je samo kuriozitet koji će završiti u podnožju neke buduće studije? Jedna stvar je sigurna: astronomija više nije znanost o *statičkim, predvidljivim objektima*. Svaka nova eksplozija, svaka neobična supernova, svaki neklasični pulsar podsjeća nas da svemir **ne voli biti stavljen u okvire**. V615 Vul je samo najnoviji primjer kako čak i *najpoznatiji* fenomeni – kao što su nove – mogu skrivati iznenađenja. A ako se ispostavi da je ovo samo vrh ledenjaka... pa, tada bismo možda trebali početi tražiti nove modele. Doslovno.
