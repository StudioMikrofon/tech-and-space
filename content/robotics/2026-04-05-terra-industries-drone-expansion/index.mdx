Terra Industries, an African start-up company, aims to produce 30,000 drones per year. The company utilizes in-house production for its drones and offers a subscription-based business model. According to available information, Terra Industries' strategy is inspired by the Ukrainian UAV revolution and Apple's business practices. [TechRadar](https://www.techradar.com/) reports that the company is expanding drone deployments across Africa, which could have significant implications for various industries such as agriculture, construction, and surveillance. 


The use of drones in these industries could increase efficiency and reduce costs. For instance, drones can be used for [crop monitoring](https://www.droneindustryinsights.com/crop-monitoring) and [construction site inspection](https://www.constructionbusinessowner.com/), allowing for real-time data collection and analysis. However, there are also concerns about [drone safety](https://www.faa.gov/uas/) and regulation, which need to be addressed.

<ArticleBreak />

The company's focus on in-house production and subscriptions could help to reduce costs and increase accessibility. However, the actual environment requirement for these drones is a critical factor that will determine their success. [Demo vs deployment](https://www.wired.com/tag/drones/) is a crucial consideration, as the conditions in which the drones will be used can be very different from those in a controlled demo. The community is responding to this development with interest, as it could potentially [disrupt traditional industries](https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbestechcouncil/2018/07/12/how-drones-are-disrupting-traditional-industries/). 


The hardware limits of the drones, such as battery life and payload capacity, will also play a significant role in determining their usefulness. As [reported by IEEE](https://spectrum.ieee.org/), the development of more efficient batteries and propulsion systems could help to increase the range and endurance of the drones. Additionally, the [scale-up friction](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/aerospace-and-defense/our-insights/the-future-of-drones) associated with mass deployment, including cost, certification, and reliability, will need to be addressed.
