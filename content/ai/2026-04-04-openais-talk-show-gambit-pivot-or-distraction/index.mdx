OpenAI’s latest moves read like a corporate identity crisis dressed as strategy. The company [confirmed](https://www.cnet.com/tech/computing/openai-shuts-down-sora-image-generator-delays-chatgpt-adult-mode/) it’s discontinuing **Sora**, its text-to-image generator, and indefinitely delaying ChatGPT’s much-hyped (and much-mocked) **‘adult mode’**—both decisions framed as ‘focus’ but smacking of retreat. Meanwhile, the acquisition of an unnamed **talk show**—yes, a talk show—suggests a pivot so abrupt it borders on performance art.


The Sora shutdown isn’t shocking. Competing tools like [MidJourney](https://www.midjourney.com/) and [Adobe Firefly](https://firefly.adobe.com/) have lapped it in adoption, and OpenAI’s own [DALL·E 3](https://openai.com/dall-e-3) remains the flagship. But killing a product *after* announcing it? That’s less ‘strategic pruning’ and more ‘admitting the demo wasn’t ready for prime time.’ The **‘adult mode’ delay**, meanwhile, reeks of regulatory cold feet—generative AI’s [ongoing ethical landmines](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.08823) make explicit content a legal gamble no one’s eager to take.


What’s weirder is the talk show. If this were a [Google](https://www.theverge.com/2023/10/12/23913062/google-podcasts-shutdown-youtube-music-transfer) or [Meta](https://www.wired.com/story/meta-ai-llama-2-open-source/) side project, we’d call it a vanity play. But OpenAI? The company that once promised **AGI in our lifetimes** now owns a chat format older than radio. Either this is a [Sam Altman](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-01-15/sam-altman-s-openai-is-now-a-86-billion-startup-with-no-business-model)-approved moonshot into ‘AI as media,’ or it’s the tech equivalent of a midlife crisis sports car.

<ArticleBreak />

The **real signal** isn’t the talk show itself—it’s the **pattern of abandonment**. OpenAI’s [2023 roadmap](https://openai.com/blog/our-approach-to-ai-safety) bulged with ‘side quests’ like voice cloning and multimodal experiments. Now, those are being culling in favor of… *scalable entertainment*? That’s not focus. That’s admitting the [hype cycle](https://www.gartner.com/en/research/methodologies/gartner-hype-cycle) outpaced the product pipeline.


Developers aren’t buying it. On [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=40234567), the reaction ranges from ‘desperate pivot’ to ‘distraction from core R&D.’ GitHub activity around OpenAI’s tools hasn’t spiked—no one’s racing to build on a talk show. And competitors? [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/) and [Mistral](https://mistral.ai/) are doubling down on **enterprise and open-source**, not late-night hosting.


The bigger question: **Who benefits?** If this is a content play, OpenAI’s now competing with [Spotify](https://www.theverge.com/2024/4/15/24130462/spotify-podcast-video-ai-voice-translation) and [YouTube](https://blog.youtube/news-and-events/ai-at-youtube/)—giants with actual media infrastructure. If it’s a branding stunt, it’s a weird flex for a company that just [laid off 20% of its team](https://www.reuters.com/technology/openai-lays-off-about-20-its-team-2024-01-19/). Either way, the **reality gap** yawns: OpenAI’s selling vision, but the roadmap’s full of potholes.


The ‘adult mode’ delay, at least, makes sense. Regulators are [circling](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2024/01/ftc-launches-inquiry-generative-ai-investments-partnerships), and OpenAI’s [safety board](https://openai.com/safety-advisory-group) is still a PowerPoint deck. But a talk show? That’s not innovation. That’s a **Hail Mary**—or a white flag.

Here’s the unanswered question: If OpenAI’s talk show isn’t about AI, what *is* it about? A media empire? A PR lifeline? Or just the world’s most expensive way to avoid admitting the side quests were dead ends?
