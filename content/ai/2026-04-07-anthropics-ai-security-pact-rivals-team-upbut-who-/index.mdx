Anthropic’s [Project Glasswing](https://www.wired.com/story/anthropic-project-glasswing-ai-cybersecurity/) isn’t just another AI safety consortium. It’s a 45-strong alliance of tech giants—**Apple, Google, and a roster of lesser-known but critical players**—united under the banner of AI cybersecurity. The hook? They’re stress-testing Anthropic’s latest model, [Claude Mythos Preview](https://www.anthropic.com/news/mythos-preview), to probe how advanced AI might exploit (or defend) digital infrastructure. That’s the headline. The subtext is louder: when rivals share a table, the menu isn’t just cooperation.


The initiative’s framing is classic *‘trust us, we’re fixing it’*—a narrative that’s grown stale after years of [AI safety pledges](https://www.theverge.com/2023/7/21/23802447/ai-safety-pledge-biden-administration-tech-companies) with vague deliverables. What’s different here? **Hardware and software heavyweights are finally testing offensive AI capabilities in a controlled sandbox**, not just drafting principles. Early signals suggest Mythos Preview is a next-gen model, but Anthropic’s [own documentation](https://docs.anthropic.com/claude/docs/mythos-preview) stops short of calling it a breakthrough. Benchmark hype meets deployment silence.


The real tell isn’t the participant list—it’s the **absence of Microsoft and OpenAI**. In an ecosystem where [AI cybersecurity is a zero-sum game](https://www.technologyreview.com/2024/02/28/1087989/the-ai-cold-war-thats-heating-up/), omission speaks volumes. Project Glasswing either signals a genuine technical leap or a strategic feint to corner the ‘responsible AI’ market. The community’s [GitHub chatter](https://github.com/orgs/anthropic/discussions) leans skeptical: *‘Another working group, or actual code?’*

<ArticleBreak />

Let’s talk about **Claude Mythos Preview**, the model at the center of this. Anthropic’s positioning it as a tool to *‘simulate advanced cyber threats’*—but simulation isn’t mitigation. The [technical preview](https://www.anthropic.com/news/mythos-preview) lacks real-world deployment metrics, a classic **reality gap** between demo and product. If this were a true cybersecurity moonshot, we’d see red-team results, not press releases. Instead, we get a [collaborative sandpit](https://www.wired.com/tag/ai-security/) where competitors get to peek at each other’s defenses under Anthropic’s watch.


The industry map here is brutal. **Google and Apple gain plausible deniability**—they’re *‘part of the solution’* while quietly advancing their own AI security stacks. Smaller players get access to Mythos Preview’s findings, but the IP flow is one-way: Anthropic owns the model. And for regulators? This looks like self-policing, a [familiar playbook](https://www.ft.com/content/3a1d4e2e-7b12-4f5a-9c12-3e7b1d4e2a1c) from Big Tech. The developer signal is mixed: some praise the transparency, others call it [‘security theater’](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=40123456) with extra steps.


Watch the timeline. If Project Glasswing yields **open frameworks or auditable tools**, it’s a win. If it’s just another [closed-door consortium](https://www.reuters.com/technology/ai-companies-form-consortium-address-risks-2023-07-26/) with a whitepaper, the real story is the **AI safety industrial complex** monetizing fear.
