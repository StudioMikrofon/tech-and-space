HarperCollins is partnering with Toonstar, an AI animation studio, to adapt some of its book franchises into digital shows. The first project under this partnership will be an adaptation of Lisa Greenwald’s *Friendship List* series, which will also include a graphic novel. Toonstar is known for simplifying traditionally complex animation pipelines with AI, but has mostly remained under the radar despite early buzz. 




The partnership implies HarperCollins may expand AI-driven adaptations beyond the *Friendship List* series in the future. Toonstar’s most notable work includes the YouTube series *StEvEn and Parker*, which has 3.38 million subscribers and occasionally reaches 1 million views per episode. [Toonstar](https://toonstar.com) has received attention for its innovative approach to animation, but its impact on the industry remains to be seen.

<ArticleBreak />

The real signal here is the potential for AI to streamline the animation process, making it more accessible and cost-effective for publishers like HarperCollins. However, the reality gap between demo and deployment is significant, and it remains to be seen whether Toonstar’s technology can deliver high-quality results at scale. [HarperCollins](https://www.harpercollins.com) is taking a calculated risk by investing in AI-driven animation, but the payoff could be substantial if it can tap into new audiences and revenue streams. 




The developer community is watching with interest, as Toonstar’s approach has the potential to disrupt traditional animation workflows and create new opportunities for creators and publishers alike. [GitHub](https://github.com) activity and technical forums suggest a growing interest in AI-driven animation tools, but it’s still early days for this technology.
