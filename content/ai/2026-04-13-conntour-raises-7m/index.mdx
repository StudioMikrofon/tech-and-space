Conntour, a startup focused on AI-powered search for security video systems, has raised $7 million in funding from General Catalyst and Y Combinator. This investment is intended to further develop Conntour's technology, which allows security teams to query camera feeds using natural language. For instance, teams can ask the system to find any object, person, or situation, streamlining the process of reviewing security footage. 


 The use of natural language processing (NLP) and computer vision enables Conntour's system to understand complex queries, making it a potentially powerful tool for security teams. According to [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/), this funding round underscores the growing interest in AI applications for security and surveillance.

<ArticleBreak />

The development of an AI-powered search engine for security video systems represents a significant step forward in video analytics. It's likely that this technology will be seen as a next-gen tool for security applications, given its potential to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of security teams. As [General Catalyst](https://www.generalcatalyst.com/) and [Y Combinator](https://www.ycombinator.com/) invest in Conntour, it signals a competitive move in the video surveillance market. The community is responding with interest, noting the potential benefits of such technology for enhancing security measures. 


 However, the real test of Conntour's system will be in its deployment and real-world performance. As [industry reports](https://www.securityinfowatch.com/) suggest, the benchmarks for such systems are high, and actual performance may vary. It's possible that the AI models used by Conntour could leverage computer vision and NLP to improve search efficiency in security footage, but this remains to be seen in practical application.
