Gimlet Labs has raised $80 million in a Series A funding round for its technology that enables AI inference to run simultaneously across NVIDIA, AMD, Intel, ARM, Cerebras, and d-Matrix chips. This development is significant as it addresses the AI inference bottleneck, a major challenge in the industry. According to [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/), the company's solution is described as 'surprisingly elegant'. 


The AI inference bottleneck refers to the limitation in processing power that hinders the efficient deployment of AI models. Gimlet Labs' technology aims to solve this problem by allowing AI to run across multiple hardware platforms. This could potentially improve efficiency or performance in AI workloads. For instance, [NVIDIA](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/) and [AMD](https://www.amd.com/en) are major players in the hardware industry, and Gimlet Labs' support for their chips could have significant implications.
