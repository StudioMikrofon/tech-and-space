Suno, an AI-powered music creation platform, is struggling to reach licensing deals with Universal Music Group and Sony Music Entertainment. The dispute centers on whether users should be allowed to share AI-generated songs they create via the platform. According to a report from the Financial Times, Universal Music Group prefers that AI-generated tracks remain confined within apps. This restriction may hinder Suno's growth or partnerships with major labels.


The information originates from a Financial Times report, cited by The Verge. The disagreement suggests Suno's business model, which allows user-generated content sharing, conflicts with major labels' licensing terms. The community is responding, with some users noting the importance of freedom to share AI-generated content.


Suno's platform uses AI to generate music, allowing users to create and share their own tracks. However, the licensing issues may delay or hinder the platform's growth. The Financial Times report highlights the challenges faced by AI-powered music creation platforms in negotiating licensing deals with major music labels.

The rise of AI-powered music creation platforms like Suno has disrupted traditional music production and distribution models. The industry map is changing, with new players emerging and traditional labels facing challenges. [Sony Music Entertainment](https://www.sony_music.com/) and Universal Music Group are among the major labels affected by this shift. The developer community is reacting, with some developers noting the potential for AI-powered music creation to democratize music production.
