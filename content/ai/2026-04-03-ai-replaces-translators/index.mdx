Warhorse Studios, the developer of Kingdom Come: Deliverance, has announced that it will be replacing human translators with AI-powered localization tools. Max Hejtmánek, a translator with 8 years of experience in the franchise, was informed of his termination due to the upcoming implementation of AI localization. The studio claims that this move will save finances and increase efficiency. 


 This decision raises questions about the impact of AI on the job market, particularly in industries where human skills are being replaced by automated systems. According to [Warhorse Studios](https://www.warhorsestudios.com/), the use of AI localization will allow for faster and more accurate translations, but it remains to be seen how this will affect the quality of the game's localization.

<ArticleBreak />

The use of AI in localization is not a new concept, but its implementation in the gaming industry is still in its early stages. While AI can process large amounts of data quickly and accurately, it lacks the nuance and cultural understanding that human translators bring to a project. As [Push Square](https://www.pushsquare.com/) notes, the success of AI localization will depend on the quality of the data used to train the system and the ability to fine-tune it for specific languages and cultures. 


 The gaming industry is watching this development closely, as it could have significant implications for the future of game localization. If successful, AI localization could reduce costs and increase efficiency, but it could also lead to job losses and a homogenization of cultural references.
