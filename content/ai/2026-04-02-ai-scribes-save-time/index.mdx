STAT News published a study on AI scribes, finding they save doctors 16 minutes per 8 hours of patient care. This modest time savings was observed across five academic medical centers. The study's findings suggest inconsistent use of AI scribes among doctors, implying variability in adoption or effectiveness. According to [STAT News](https://www.statnews.com/), the time savings from AI scribes were described as 'modest' in the study’s findings.


The study was conducted by STAT News and involved five academic medical centers, although the names of these centers were not specified. [AI scribe technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_scribe) has been touted as a solution to reduce doctors' workload, but its actual impact is still being studied.

<ArticleBreak />

The real signal here is that AI scribes are not a magic solution to reduce doctors' workload. While they do offer some time savings, the benefits are not as significant as some might have hoped. As [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/) notes, the use of AI in healthcare is still in its early stages, and more research is needed to fully understand its potential. The community is responding with a mix of optimism and skepticism, with some players noting that AI scribes could be a valuable tool, but others expressing concerns about their limitations.


The study's findings have implications for the healthcare industry, particularly for companies developing AI scribe technology. As [Wired](https://www.wired.com/) reports, the market for AI in healthcare is growing rapidly, and companies that can develop effective solutions will be well-positioned to take advantage of this trend.
