Researchers have developed a generative modeling framework that enables petascale synthesis of designed DNA. This framework is described as 'manufacturing-aware' and allows for large-scale designed DNA synthesis, yielding candidate cell therapies. According to [Nature Biotechnology](https://www.nature.com/nbt/), the study was published online on March 17, 2026, with a DOI of 10.1038/s41587-026-03020-8. The implications of this study are significant, as it could potentially lead to new cell therapies.


The study's findings are based on a generative modeling framework that takes into account the manufacturing process, allowing for more efficient and accurate DNA synthesis. This approach has the potential to accelerate the development of new cell therapies, which could have a significant impact on the field of medicine. As noted by [The National Institutes of Health](https://www.nih.gov/), cell therapies have shown promise in treating a range of diseases and conditions.
