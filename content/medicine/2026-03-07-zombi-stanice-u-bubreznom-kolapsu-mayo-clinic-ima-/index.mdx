Researchers at the Mayo Clinic have identified a potential treatment for diabetic kidney disease, which affects millions of people worldwide. The team found that a combination of the cancer drug dasatinib and a naturally occurring substance called quercetin can reduce the harmful effects of senescent cells, also known as 'zombie cells', in the kidneys. According to the study published in [eBioMedicine](https://www.ebiomedicine.com/), the combination therapy decreased inflammation and boosted protective factors in the kidney. This is a significant finding, as diabetic kidney disease is a leading cause of kidney failure and death. The study's lead author noted that the findings are promising, but more research is needed to confirm the results. As reported by [MedicalXpress](https://medicalxpress.com/), the study's results are based on a small sample size and more research is needed to fully understand the potential benefits and limitations of the treatment.

&lt;ArticleBreak />

The study's findings are based on a small sample size, which is a limitation of the research. However, the results are promising and suggest that the combination therapy may be a potential treatment option for diabetic kidney disease. The researchers used a rigorous methodology, including [randomized controlled trials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randomized_controlled_trial) to test the effectiveness of the treatment. The study's results are also supported by previous research on the effects of senescent cells on kidney function. As noted by [the Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/), more research is needed to fully understand the potential benefits and limitations of the treatment. The study's findings have been reported by several reputable sources, including [ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/) and [HealthDay](https://www.healthday.com/).
