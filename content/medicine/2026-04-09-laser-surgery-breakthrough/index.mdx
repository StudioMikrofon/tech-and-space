Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) researchers have developed a flexible optical fiber designed for laser surgery of voice box tumors, specifically targeting vocal folds. This technology can be threaded through a medical endoscope and steered into the larynx to reach tumors in hard-to-access areas. The research was published in the Journal of Medical Devices. According to [WPI](https://www.wpi.edu/), this advance could expand outpatient laser treatment options for patients who might otherwise require surgery under general anesthesia.


  The team's work is focused on improving treatment options for patients with voice box tumors, and [MedicalXpress](https://medicalxpress.com/) reports that the flexible optical fiber can be used to destroy hard-to-reach tumors on the vocal folds. This is a significant development, as it could reduce the need for invasive procedures and improve patient outcomes. As noted by [Journal of Medical Devices](https://medicalxpress.com/journal/journal-of-medical-devices), the research is an important step forward in the treatment of laryngeal cancer.

<ArticleBreak />

While this technology shows promise, it is essential to consider the limitations of the study. The sample size and methodology used in the research have not been disclosed, and [further studies](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) are needed to confirm the efficacy of this treatment. Additionally, the mechanism of steering the fiber into the larynx is not fully understood, and more research is needed to determine the safety and effectiveness of this procedure. As reported by [WPI](https://www.wpi.edu/news), the researchers are cautiously optimistic about the potential of this technology to improve treatment options for patients with voice box tumors.


  The development of this flexible optical fiber is a significant step forward in the treatment of laryngeal cancer, and [experts](https://www.healthline.com/) are eagerly awaiting further research on this topic. The potential to reduce reliance on invasive procedures and improve patient outcomes is substantial, and this technology could have a significant impact on the field of medicine. With [continued research](https://www.medlineplus.gov/) and development, this technology could become a valuable tool in the treatment of voice box tumors.
