The FDA has rejected an industry proposal to deregulate certain types of AI devices, [STAT News](https://www.statnews.com/) reports. This decision is significant as it affects the development and deployment of AI in healthcare. According to [FDA guidelines](https://www.fda.gov/), the agency is responsible for ensuring the safety and efficacy of medical devices, including those that use AI.


The proposal in question targeted the deregulation of specific AI devices, which would have allowed them to be marketed and used without undergoing the same level of scrutiny as other medical devices. However, the FDA's decision suggests that the agency is taking a cautious approach to the regulation of AI in healthcare, recognizing the potential risks and benefits associated with these technologies.


As [reported by STAT News](https://www.statnews.com/), the pharmaceutical industry has invested $50 million in Digital Therapeutics (DTx), which is a related field that uses digital technologies to treat medical conditions. This investment highlights the growing interest in using technology to improve healthcare outcomes, but also underscores the need for careful regulation to ensure that these technologies are safe and effective.

<ArticleBreak />

The FDA's decision to reject the proposal to deregulate AI devices is a reminder that the development and deployment of AI in healthcare must be carefully regulated to ensure patient safety. As [noted by experts](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/), the use of AI in healthcare has the potential to improve outcomes, but it also raises important questions about safety, efficacy, and equity.


In the context of DTx, the investment by the pharmaceutical industry highlights the potential for digital technologies to transform the way we treat medical conditions. However, as [reported by [Nature](https://www.nature.com/)], the development of DTx also raises important questions about regulation, reimbursement, and access to care.


The FDA's decision to reject the proposal to deregulate AI devices is an important step towards ensuring that the development and deployment of AI in healthcare is done in a way that prioritizes patient safety and well-being. As [stated by the FDA](https://www.fda.gov/), the agency is committed to supporting innovation in healthcare while also ensuring that new technologies are safe and effective.
